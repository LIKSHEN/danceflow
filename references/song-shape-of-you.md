## Choreography Notes

- **96 BPM** = moderate tempo, good for hip-hop groove with half-time feel
- Strong rhythmic accents on beats 2 and 4 (snare pattern)
- Pre-chorus sections are ideal for formation transitions (16s build window)
- Bridge section works well for intimate/circle formations before final chorus explosion
- Outro naturally suits reforming starting formation or final pose

## Formation Progression Used (5 dancers, hip-hop)

1. Intro → Straight line (starting positions)
2. Verse 1 → Wide arc (spread out, D3 slightly forward)
3. Pre-Chorus → V-shape (D3 as point, build energy)
4. Chorus 1 → Split groups with center power (D3 forward)
5. Verse 2 → Diagonal line (dynamic angle)
6. Pre-Chorus 2 → Diamond formation (compact, ready to explode)
7. Chorus 2 → Wide spread (maximum stage usage)
8. Bridge → Circle/arc (intimate, connected)
9. Chorus 3 → Pyramid (peak energy, D3 at apex)
10. Outro → Reform straight line, final pose

## Movement Vocabulary Applied

- Intro: isolations (head rolls, shoulder pops, chest pops), finger snaps, weight shifts
- Verses: groove walks, body rolls, arm waves, mirror pairs (D1↔D5, D2↔D4)
- Pre-Choruses: arm pumps (sync), fast chest pops, step-slides to formation positions
- Choruses: jumps, power slides, unison combo hits, arm cascades, spins
- Bridge: partner moves, energy passing, slow-motion freezes
- Outro: slow body rolls, group sync leans, final snap + freeze
